Future Outlook
Where It Is Headed

Most of the future currently planned for VR technology revolves around general improvements to existing flaws. Clipping through virtual surfaces has been an issue when making solids interactable for the user, so resolving that is of top priority. Other items on the agenda include making certain headsets cable-less, adding more powerful processors and potentially even A.I. Accessibility is also a major target, both in the monetary sense as well as for people who have trouble using headsets as is, like for people with motion-sickness.

While there are certainly factors that hold VR technology back, none of these hurdles are impossible to overcome, and doing so will reap a myriad of benefits. This is especially apparent for the gaming industry, which is estimated to reach a value of $2.5 billion in the U.S. by the end of 2023, with $161.1 billion expected from all fields worldwide when considering VR and AR together.
